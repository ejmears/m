Elijah J. Mears

Writer, Composer, Philologist, Poet.


Elijah Jacob Mears, born January 3, 1995, is a lifelong resident of North Carolina currently residing in Greensboro. He attends UNCG studying Classical Languages & Literature and Archaeology, where he is a member of UNCG Hillel and an officer of the UNCG Classical Society. He writes weekly for Odyssey, an online content discovery platform, and has been published in The Coraddi, UNCG’s literary magazine. Additionally, he has presented papers as part of academic conferences at UNC Greensboro and the University of Tennessee, and has been invited to present his paper Le Nozze di Cicerone: Marriage Metaphor in the Second Philippic at the CAMWS Southern Section conference this October.

His interests within Classics include Latin linguistics and Vulgar Latin, the Late Roman Republic, and Roman epistolary literature. He was recognized cum honore maximo egregio for his perfect score on the Level VI National Latin Exam in Spring 2015, and he received a certificate of commendation for his high score on the CAMWS Latin Translation Exam in poetry in Fall 2015. Other interests include composing classical music, fantasy and sci-fi literature, conlanging, programming, and politics. As a writer, he cites JRR Tolkien, Douglas Adams, Brian Jacques, and Lemony Snicket as particular influences. He is also a Ravenclaw.
